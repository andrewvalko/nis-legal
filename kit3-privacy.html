Privacy Policy — KIT3

Last updated: July 20, 2026

KIT3 ("the App") is a small photo-exchange app for staying in touch with close friends, operated by Andrew Valko. This policy explains what data the App handles and how.

What we collect

  • Account: the App uses anonymous sign-in — we do not collect your email address, password, phone number, or real name. Your account is an anonymous identifier plus the display name you choose.
  • Photos: the photos you choose to send to your friends each week. Photos are only ever visible to the specific friend(s) you send them to.
  • Weekly status (optional): short free-text notes you may add — how you're feeling, what you're listening to, and a location note you type yourself. The App does not access your device's GPS or location services.
  • Invites: invite links you create or redeem to connect with a friend.
  • Push tokens: a device token so we can send you notifications (e.g. when a friend's photos unlock).

What we don't do

  • No advertising, ever. No advertising identifiers.
  • No sale or sharing of your data with third parties for their own purposes.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • No access to your contacts, camera roll (beyond the photos you explicitly pick), microphone, or device location.

Who can see your content

Photos and statuses are shared only with the friends you have an explicit, mutual connection with in the App ("KITs"). There is no public feed, no discovery, and no way for strangers to see your content.

Where data is stored

Data is stored and processed using Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Cloud Storage, Cloud Messaging), acting as our processor. See Google's privacy policy. Data is transmitted over encrypted connections.

Retention and deletion

Photos follow the App's weekly cadence and older photo drops may be periodically deleted. You can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time by emailing Andrew — we'll complete it within 30 days.

Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, we'll update this page and note it in the App's release notes.
