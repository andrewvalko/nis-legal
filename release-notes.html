Release history

Each version lists what shipped in that Android release. iOS tracks the same cadence.

v1.6.9

Meet the members

The Stats tab is now called Club, and there's a new "View all members" button at the bottom. Tap it to see a face-grid of everyone in the club — recently active members up top, the rest below — so you can put names to faces. Tap any avatar to jump to that person's profile.

The feed updates itself

When someone else logs a swim, edits one, or creates an event, your feed now refreshes on its own — no more pull-to-refresh to see what's new. Same for comments and reactions on swims you have open. (This was a longstanding bug under the hood; happy it's finally fixed.)

Approved? You're in.

New members previously had to kill and reopen the app after an admin approved them. Now the moment your status flips, the app drops you into the feed automatically.

Recent comments and fist bumps

Feed cards now show the three most recent comments instead of the oldest three, and name the most recent fist-bumper. Small thing, big freshness boost.

"30h" instead of "1d"

The Next Up countdown on Events stays in hours through 36h before flipping to "1d" — a swim 30 hours away isn't really "tomorrow," and now it doesn't say so.

Polish

Tab pill no longer covers the bottom of any scrolling list. The jellyfish chip on the feed hero is readable in dark mode again. iOS gets the swim-saved toast back. Members directory avatars 4pt larger. Event-saved toast on the Events tab now matches the swim-saved toast on the Feed.

v1.6.7

Event detail, redesigned

Event detail now matches the look the swim detail got last release: a gradient hero with the day, time, date and location up top; the title and description peek out from a subtle card underneath; and a friendly "Going" section with chips for everyone who's RSVP'd, with the organizer marked.

"Up Next" event card polish

The Up Next card on the feed no longer truncates the countdown ("2 days from now") or the RSVP button, and shows up to four faces with a "+N" tile if more are going.

v1.6.6

Swim detail, redesigned

A new look for the swim detail screen. Photos go full-bleed with a paging strip when you have more than one. When there's no photo, a "stats hero" card leads with distance and water temp at hero scale. Below the hero, your meta line and notes peek out on a subtle card. Achievements get their own row treatment, fist bumps render as cleaner chips with the reaction button up in the section heading, and the comments thread + composer match.

Fist bumps stop "disappearing"

Two real bugs: the feed pagination was wiping reactions on the first page when you scrolled, and a transient network blip on cold start could leave every fist bump rendering un-highlighted. Both fixed — your reactions stay highlighted across sessions and as you scroll.

84-day stats period

The Stats and Leaderboard time-period chips now include 84d (12 weeks) alongside 7d / 28d / 56d.

Cleaner "After that" event cards

The day-number column got wider and recolored to deep navy so dates like "29" don't wrap. Subtitles now always include the location (e.g. "12 pm · Sandkaj"). The trailing comment row is gone — tap the card to see comments on the detail screen.

Tap the comment icon, get the keyboard

Tapping the comment bubble on a swim feed card now opens the swim detail view with the comment field already focused and the keyboard up — one tap fewer to reply.

Dark mode polish

The "NEXT UP" pill on the events tab is visible again in dark mode (was blending into the card behind it). Personal Records stat cells got a subtler treatment that reads cleanly in both light and dark.

v1.6.5

Dark mode, back for real

Dark mode now adapts every screen, card, face pile, and stat block — no more stark white slabs or invisible text. Matches your phone's system setting automatically.

Event attendees show the organizer

Face piles on upcoming events now include the organizer and show up to 8 faces. The count next to the pile reads as "Tim B. and 3 others" so you can see who's coming at a glance.

Cleaner swim detail

The Achievements header on a swim no longer has a heavy blue band — it reads as a subtle subhead now, matching the Fist Bumps section below it.

Feed face pile polish

Fist-bump face piles show up to 6 avatars (was 4), and each avatar has a subtle ring so overlapping faces stay distinct.

v1.6.4

New Events tab

Upcoming events now open with a big hero card for the next one, with a clean timeline of what's after. Tap any event for the full story.

See who fist-bumped

Every swim now shows faces of the people who fist-bumped it, right next to the count. See at a glance who's been celebrating your swims.

Cleaner swim cards

Labels and achievements now sit together inside the card, with the comment affordance moved to the right. Everything feels tidier and more scannable.

Season goal at a glance

Your season goal progress peeks out from under the stats card at the top of the feed — always visible, one tap to edit.

v1.6.3

Smoother first paint

Opening the app no longer flashes the empty state before data loads — the feed now shows a skeleton that matches the real layout on the very first frame.

Stats lives in Your Activity

The separate personal stats card is replaced with a subtle row inside the Your Activity heatmap. Tapping it reliably opens your Stats tab.

v1.6.2

Stats tab on your profile

Your profile now has a dedicated Stats tab with your personal swim history, temperature trends, and weekly rhythm.

Fist bumps survive logout

Fixed a bug where fist bumps you'd given others appeared un-highlighted every time you logged back in.

Feed card polish

Swim labels now appear above the date/duration line, and club achievement badges no longer overlap the section title.

Photo width fix

Landscape photos no longer overflow their card boundaries in the feed.

v1.6.0

Goals

Set yourself a weekly, monthly, or season goal — number of swims or total distance — and track progress from the feed and profile. The progress bar flashes green when you log a swim that moves you forward.

Swim labels

Tag a swim with what made it notable: jellyfish, strong current, algae, cold shock. Labels show up as small chips on your swim and in the club summary, so the next person launching sees a heads-up before heading in.

Pinch-to-explore scatter plot

Tap any scatter chart to open it fullscreen. Avatars replace the dots so you can see who swam where, pinch to zoom into a cluster, and tap any point to jump straight to that swim.

Seasonal achievements

Most achievements now reset each Oct 1 so there's something to chase every winter. New tiers for unique swim buddies and single-swim distance (Big Dip, Ice Mile, Marathon Dip). May–Sept swims are off-season and don't count.

Club summary, redesigned

One hero period (Today, or the most recent day with activity) now leads with three headline stats — avg temp, swims, distance — and secondary periods fold into a single inline line underneath. Empty days no longer take up space.

Fixes

Longest Swim leaderboard respects the period filter again. The Correlations chart temperature axis fits the data instead of clipping. Buddy swim filters match the rollup consistently, and a few small thread-safety gremlins in the feed are gone.

v1.5.6

Light app redesign with NIS blue

The whole app got a visual refresh built around the NIS snowflake blue. Bolder header bands, white cards on a soft frost background, larger toolbar with the snowflake as a tap target, and consistent rounded corners across every surface.

Compact profile header

Avatar and name now sit side by side; email and "Change Color" share a line. More room for your stats and achievements above the fold.

Cleaner forms

Tapping a number field with existing content (distance, water temp, duration) now selects all so you can just type the new value. Smoother save buttons and tighter input spacing throughout.

More Danish, more accessible

Pending-approval screen, admin tools, and the section headers across the app are now translated to Danish. Accessibility labels (Back, More, Menu, recurring event) read in Danish for VoiceOver / TalkBack instead of English.

Polish everywhere

Smoother transitions, better contrast on bands and chips, the "Comments" header reads as a quieter subhead, swim photos get a soft drop-shadow from the header band, and a long list of small spacing/typography fixes.

v1.5.5

Real-time everything

Comments, reactions, photo uploads, avatars, unread counts, pending-approval badges, upcoming events, and per-swim edits all update live via Firestore listeners. No more pull-to-refresh for normal updates.

Multi-photo swims

Up to 3 photos per swim. New peek carousel — current photo full-bleed with the next one peeking from the trailing edge (Strava-style). Edit-swim shows existing photos and lets you remove them; selection order is preserved.

Pending-approval admin badges

Admin shield in the toolbar gets a red badge when members are waiting for approval. Server-side notifications + cleanup when a user is approved.

Better upload feedback

Photo uploads await inline with a visible "Saving photos…" toast. Avatar upload polling is tighter. Toast styles unified.

Inbox polish

Inbox rows now fill the card width and share the same container style as Feed and Events, and read notifications drop off on their own after three days so the list doesn't pile up.

Smoother screen transitions (Android)

Navigating between screens on Android now uses proper slide and fade transitions instead of hard cuts, matching the feel iOS has always had.

v1.5.4

Bigger tap targets on swim log

Form field rows on the swim log are easier to hit on both platforms. Min/sec labels are independently tap-focusable.

Faster everywhere

Parallelized Firestore reads, fixed N+1 patterns, and improved caching across feed, profile, and stats.

Name auto-capitalize

First and last names auto-capitalize during sign-up on both platforms.

Notification & profile polish

Notification badge clears after marking read. Public profile visual style matches own-profile. Dark mode fixes on summary card chip and background.

v1.5.3

New swim log fields

Warm-up / sauna time and real feel temperature. Real feel appears when you toggle air temp on. Both show on swim detail.

Club achievements on feed

Club achievements surface as chips in the Club summary card header. Tap to jump to the swim that earned it.

v1.5.2

Edit your swims

Menu on your own swims lets you edit notes, buddies, and photos any time. Stats-affecting fields lock after 24 hours to protect leaderboards, with a clear disabled state.

In-place detail refresh

After an edit, the swim detail view refreshes in place instead of popping back to the feed.

v1.5.1

Redesigned mini-events

Mini-event items on the swim feed were redesigned. Event cards show individual attendees and live RSVP counts on Android too.

FlowLayout fist-bumps

Fist-bump lists use compact chips that wrap naturally instead of a rigid grid.

Club totals + notification polish

Club feed totals only count official Sandkaj swims. Notification body strings are fully localized (en/da) and standardized.

v1.5.0

@Mentions

Type @ in comments or swim notes to tag a member. Mentions render bold inline, trigger a notification, and have their own Settings toggles. Picker adapts to dark/light mode and small screens. Cursor-aware detection and atomic delete on backspace.

Splash & keyboard fixes

Splash wordplay animation is always visible. Swim log no longer jumps to the bottom when the keyboard opens; notes field stays in view.

Avatar re-upload fix

Avatar changes bust the image cache properly and update across the app on both platforms.

v1.4.1

Real-time feed

Feed pagination plus live feed updates. New swims and reactions show up without pull-to-refresh.

Tab bar + FAB

Floating action button integrated into the tab bar. iOS uses the native iOS 26 Liquid Glass tab bar; Android matches the spacing.

Accurate RSVP counts

RSVP counts fetch real attendee lists instead of a denormalized number that could drift.

Spacing & density

Feed and card spacing bumped +2pt for breathing room. Event and chart cards match swim feed density.

v1.4.0

Air temp & water time

Two optional fields on the swim log. Air temp is behind a snowflake toggle; water time counts for charts and leaderboards.

Tabbed profile on Android

Android profile splits into Overview / Swims / Settings to match iOS. Admin shield + tools overlay lands on Android too.

Redesigned forms

Swim log and event creation got a SectionCard-based overhaul — cleaner grouping, lock/clock affordances on locked fields.

Write-in buddies

Tag non-members as swim buddies by typing their name. Displays even when they don't have an account.

Visual polish

System fonts across both platforms. Card borders removed on Android to match iOS. Consistent 6pt subhead spacing. Reordered profile sections. Mini-events use a two-column layout.

v1.3.5

Dark mode

Follows your phone's system setting. Every screen, chart, and card adapted on both platforms.

Swim photos

Attach a photo when logging a swim. Shows as a hero image on your feed card, with a fade-in on load.

Profile photos

Upload a real avatar. Replaces the colored circle everywhere — feed, comments, leaderboards.

Achievements on profile

Earned badges show in a grid on your profile. Others can see them too, with a progress bar for the next unlock.

Stats tab overhaul

Four charts: trends, temperature correlations, progression, and a heatmap. Adaptive bucketing, rich flyouts, sticky period filters.

Announcements

Admin-authored announcements with unread green outline, avatar + name header, bold/italic support, 2000-char limit, show-more truncation, and admin delete.

Events upgrades

Weekly look-ahead strip on the Events tab. Events show up to 1h after start. Cleaner cards with better time display and organizer chips.

Welcome & Help

2-page welcome NUX for new members. About & Help screen with contextual help topics throughout the app.

Danish by default

Follows your phone's language automatically. Labels, achievements, dates, and plurals all in Danish.

Haptic feedback

A little buzz on fist-bumps, logging swims, and unlocking achievements.

Faster stats

Stats load from server-side aggregation with transparent caching. Feed uses stale-while-revalidate with smart revalidation via a feedVersion counter.

Splash prefetch

Skeleton loaders

Shimmering skeletons replace spinners on events, stats, public profile, and inbox.

Offline banner

Global offline indicator when connectivity drops, localized in en/da.

Private swims

Admin tools

Reprocess Achievements on a single swim or across all users. Admin-only fields editable in Firestore rules. Password reveal toggle on login.
